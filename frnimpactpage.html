Case Study: Food Recovery Network

Food Recovery Network Impact Webpage

ENG 100D · University of California San Diego · Fall 2024

Overview

Redesigned an interactive, accessible, and emotionally resonant webpage for UCSD's Food Recovery Network (FRN) to help students facing food insecurity and to attract new partners and volunteers.

Problem

Students in need were unaware of FRN services. The existing site lacked impact storytelling, clarity, and interactive elements, limiting FRN’s visibility and ability to grow support.

Solution

We designed an engaging, accessible, and interactive webpage that highlights FRN’s mission, impact, and opportunities for involvement. Key features like a food calculator, visual maps, and student stories increased engagement and awareness.

My Role

  • UX Designer & Documentarian
  • Created wireframes and visual layouts in Figma
  • Led content strategy and drafted student testimonial layout
  • Conducted usability testing and synthesized insights

Research & Discovery

  • Interviewed students, FRN staff, and food partners
  • Surveyed food insecurity experiences and web usability
  • Benchmarked against 4 leading nonprofit impact pages

Design Features

  • Impact Calculator: Translates food saved into meals and dollars
  • Interactive Candyland Map: Gamified food recovery journey
  • Student Testimonials: Empathy-driven storytelling
  • Distribution Map & Menu: Real-time food info for students
  • Partner Recognition: Highlights sponsors and donation opportunities

Tools Used

Figma · Wix · Google Forms · HTML/CSS · Canva

Outcome

  • 8/10 usability score from testers
  • Enhanced user understanding and emotional engagement
  • Low-cost, scalable solution ready for implementation

Lessons Learned

  • Design must balance storytelling with functionality
  • Student voices and stakeholder alignment are essential
  • Interactive elements dramatically improve engagement
